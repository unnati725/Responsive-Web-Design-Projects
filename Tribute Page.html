Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam

The Missile Man of India and the 11th President of India.

Life and Achievements

  • 1931 - Born in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, India.
  • 1960 - Graduated in Aerospace Engineering from Madras Institute of Technology.
  • 1969 - Joined the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO).
  • 1980 - Played a key role in launching India's first satellite launch vehicle (SLV-III).
  • 1998 - Contributed significantly to the Pokhran-II nuclear tests.
  • 2002–2007 - Served as the 11th President of India.
  • 1997 - Awarded the Bharat Ratna, India's highest civilian honor.
  • 2015 - Passed away while delivering a lecture to students, continuing his passion for education until his final day.

Dr. Kalam inspired millions through his dedication to science, education, innovation, and national development. His vision for a developed India continues to motivate young people around the world.
